# Demand Execution Pack

## Objective

Book 20 qualified conversations, recruit five active design partners, and obtain two explicit $29/workspace/month willingness-to-pay confirmations. Do not build Phase 2 until this gate is met.

Use this preview in every CTA: <https://openapi-studio-tanstack-preview.pibin.workers.dev>

## Prospect Criteria

Contact only people who meet all four conditions:

1. Work on a software team of roughly 2–30 engineers.
2. Maintain an OpenAPI 3.0/3.1 file today.
3. Participate in editing, reviewing, publishing, or consuming that contract.
4. Can describe a recent workflow problem or introduce its owner.

Prioritize founders, backend leads, platform engineers, API designers, and developer-experience leads. Exclude students, inactive projects, generic API enthusiasts, and teams seeking only free documentation hosting.

Useful public signals: an OpenAPI file in a repository, API-documentation complaints, manual YAML review, generated-client drift, broken documentation, or recent API platform work.

## Direct Outreach

Initial message:

```text
Hi [first name]—I noticed [specific OpenAPI signal]. I’m researching how small teams handle real spec changes. Could I ask how your last change moved from edit to review and publish? I’m not asking you to buy anything; I’m looking for a concrete workflow conversation.
```

If they confirm the problem:

```text
That is exactly the workflow I’m testing. I’m recruiting five design partners for an early OpenAPI workspace. Would a 20-minute walkthrough next week be reasonable? Pilot details: https://openapi-studio-tanstack-preview.pibin.workers.dev
```

One follow-up after four business days:

```text
Quick follow-up, then I’ll close the loop: is OpenAPI maintenance an active problem for your team, or not a priority now? Either answer is useful.
```

No further follow-up without a reply.

## Daily Operating Loop

- Identify 10 qualified prospects from public evidence.
- Send 10 personalized messages; never mass-send identical DMs.
- Publish at most one channel-appropriate post.
- Reply to interested prospects the same day.
- Record private notes outside Git; update aggregate counts only in `design-partner-operating-ledger.md`.
- After five conversations, report repeated problems and objections before changing copy.

## Stop Rules

- After 50 qualified messages with fewer than five replies: rewrite the opening message.
- After 10 conversations without one repeated urgent problem: narrow or change the buyer.
- After 20 conversations without two $29 confirmations: revise the offer or price before building more.
- Never interpret likes, impressions, or unqualified signups as demand.
