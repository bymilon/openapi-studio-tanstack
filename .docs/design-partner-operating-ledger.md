# Design-Partner Operating Ledger

Track aggregates only. Keep names, emails, company details, and conversation notes in a private approved system—not Git.

## Qualified Prospect

A qualified prospect is part of a small software team that actively maintains an OpenAPI 3.0/3.1 document and can describe a recurring editing, review, or publishing problem. Exclude students, generic newsletter signups, agencies without an active contract, and people seeking free API documentation hosting only.

## Weekly Loop

1. Contact a narrow cohort using one problem-specific message.
2. Ask about the current workflow before describing features.
3. Record the exact recurring problem, current workaround, frequency, consequence, buyer, and willingness to pay privately.
4. Update only aggregate counts and repeated themes below.
5. Change positioning only after a pattern appears across at least five qualified conversations.

## Conversation Prompts

- Walk me through the last OpenAPI change your team made.
- Where did the workflow slow down or fail?
- How often does that happen, and what does it cost in time or trust?
- What have you already tried?
- Who owns this problem and who could approve a $49 monthly workspace tool?
- What must work before paying would be reasonable?

## Commercial Gate

Do not advance to identity because the page exists. Target 20 qualified conversations, five active design partners, and two explicit willingness-to-pay confirmations. If the same urgent problem does not repeat, revise the buyer or positioning before adding product surface.
