## Overview

OpenAPI Studio should feel like a calibrated review instrument in a bright engineering workspace: exact, quiet, and ready for consequential work. Use a true-white canvas, disciplined grid, confident cobalt, sharp hierarchy, and authentic contract artifacts. The system borrows enterprise clarity and evidence density—not another company’s trade dress.

## Colors

Use a restrained strategy: white and cool-neutral surfaces carry most of the page; cobalt identifies decisions and actions; signal green appears only for truthful validation or completion states. Ink must retain at least 7:1 contrast on the canvas. Avoid gradients, warm paper tones, decorative glows, and large alternating color slabs.

## Typography

Use a neutral system sans until a deliberately licensed and self-hosted family is approved. Hierarchy comes from size, weight, width, and whitespace—not monospace costume or extreme tracking. Display tracking never exceeds `-0.04em`; body copy stays within 65–72 characters and uses pretty wrapping. Use monospace only inside genuine OpenAPI artifacts.

## Elevation

The system is structurally flat. Separate layers with spacing, subtle cool surfaces, and one-pixel rules. Do not combine soft wide shadows with borders. Floating navigation or overlays may use a compact, functional shadow only when stacking must be communicated.

## Components

Navigation is compact and stable with one emphasized pilot action. Primary buttons use cobalt with white text, visible focus, and at least 44px height; secondary actions use a single rule without decorative shadow. Product artifacts show real or explicitly labelled sample OpenAPI content, changes, and validation states. Avoid generic feature cards, ornamental pills, repeated arrows, and icon-heading-text grids.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do pair claims with real source, sample-contract, validation, or delivery evidence.
- Do disclose that the editor workflow is being shaped with five design partners.
- Do vary section composition according to its narrative job while preserving one grid.
- Do test desktop, mobile, keyboard, reduced motion, JavaScript-disabled rendering, and 200% zoom.
- Don't imitate Apple, Google, GitHub, Stripe, or Linear layouts, fonts, motifs, or wording.
- Don't fabricate screenshots, customer proof, security certifications, speed metrics, or availability.
- Don't use cream, moss, terracotta, radial glow, repeated uppercase kickers, or numbered card scaffolds.
